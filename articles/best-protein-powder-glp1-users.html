Best Protein Powders for GLP-1 Users (2025)

On Ozempic or Wegovy, you're eating significantly less — which is the point. But eating less means you're also getting less protein. That's a problem, because GLP-1 medications can accelerate muscle loss alongside fat loss if you're not actively hitting your protein targets.

The bottom line: Most GLP-1 users need 100–150g of protein per day. With a suppressed appetite eating maybe 1,200–1,500 calories, that's nearly impossible from food alone. A quality protein powder fills the gap.

What to look for in a protein powder on GLP-1

Not all protein powders work well for GLP-1 users. Because your stomach empties more slowly on semaglutide, you want:

  • High protein-to-calorie ratio — you can't afford empty calories
  • Fast-digesting formula — whey isolate or hydrolyzed whey sits better on a slow stomach
  • Low fat and fiber — these slow digestion further and can worsen nausea
  • Good mixability — you're often making small shakes, it needs to dissolve cleanly

How much protein do you actually need on GLP-1?

The general recommendation for people on GLP-1 medications is 0.7–1g of protein per pound of bodyweight per day. For a 180lb person, that's 126–180g of protein daily.

On a restricted appetite eating 1,200–1,500 calories, getting even 120g from whole food alone is nearly impossible. One or two protein shakes per day bridges that gap without adding significant calories.
