Best Fat Burner Supplements 2025: What Actually Works

The fat burner supplement industry is worth billions — and most of it is built on marketing, not science. That said, a handful of ingredients have genuine research supporting their role in fat metabolism. Here's the honest breakdown of what works, what's marginal, and what's a waste of money.

Reality check: No supplement burns significant fat on its own. The best fat burners increase calorie burn by 50–100 calories per day max. They work at the margins of a calorie deficit — not instead of one.

Ingredients with actual evidence

Before looking at products, know what ingredients are worth paying for:

  • Caffeine — increases metabolic rate 3–11%, improves fat oxidation during exercise. Most effective ingredient in any fat burner.
  • Green tea extract (EGCG) — works synergistically with caffeine to increase fat oxidation. ~200–400mg EGCG is the studied dose.
  • L-Carnitine — helps transport fatty acids into mitochondria for burning. Most effective in people who are deficient (vegetarians, older adults).
  • Capsaicin — from chili peppers. Temporarily increases metabolic rate and reduces appetite. Small but real effect.
  • Yohimbine — blocks alpha-2 receptors that inhibit fat breakdown. Effective especially for stubborn fat areas. Caution: raises heart rate and blood pressure.

What to skip entirely

  • Raspberry ketones — no human evidence, only in vitro and rat studies at massive doses
  • Garcinia Cambogia — repeatedly failed to show meaningful effects in well-designed human trials
  • Anything with a "proprietary blend" — you can't verify the doses, which makes it impossible to evaluate
  • Products promising 10+ lbs/week — impossible without medication or dangerous methods
