Best Multivitamins 2025

A good multivitamin fills nutritional gaps — it doesn't replace a good diet. The problem is most multivitamins use cheap, poorly-absorbed forms of vitamins and minerals that pass through your system with minimal benefit. The difference between a $10 and $40 multivitamin is often in the form of the nutrients, not just the branding.

What to look for: Methylfolate (not folic acid), methylcobalamin B12 (not cyanocobalamin), magnesium glycinate (not oxide), vitamin D3 (not D2). These forms absorb significantly better.
