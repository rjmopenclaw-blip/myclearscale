High Protein Low Calorie Foods: The Complete Guide

Whether you're on a GLP-1 medication, following intermittent fasting, or just trying to lose weight without losing muscle, the goal is the same: maximize protein per calorie. These foods deliver the most protein for the fewest calories — making them the backbone of any effective fat loss diet.

The metric that matters: Protein efficiency = grams of protein per 100 calories. Foods scoring 15+ are exceptional. These are the ones to build your diet around.

🏆 The Top Tier (Score 15+)

Canned Tuna in Water
Score: 25.9

30g protein per 116 calories. The king of protein efficiency. Cheap, portable, no prep required. Wild-caught skipjack is lowest in mercury for frequent consumption.

Shrimp (Cooked)
Score: 24.2

24g protein / 99 calories per 100g. Zero carbs, almost no fat. Freezer-friendly and versatile. One of the most calorie-efficient protein sources on Earth.

Turkey Breast (Cooked)
Score: 21.5

29g protein / 135 calories. Slightly leaner than chicken breast. Deli-sliced turkey is one of the most accessible high-protein, low-calorie options for quick meals.

Egg Whites
Score: 20.6

26g protein / 126 calories per cup. Zero fat, zero carbs. Liquid egg whites from cartons make prep trivial — add to scrambled eggs or protein shakes to boost protein without calories.

Chicken Breast (Cooked)
Score: 18.8

31g protein / 165 calories. The classic for a reason. Versatile, widely available, and consistently the highest protein density among common meats. The breast is significantly leaner than thighs.

Non-Fat Greek Yogurt
Score: 17.0

17g protein / 100 calories per 170g serving. Probiotics, calcium, and protein in one. Pairs with berries for a meal or snack. Fage Total 0% and Chobani Non-Fat are top picks.

Cottage Cheese (Low-Fat)
Score: 15.6

14g protein / 90 calories per ½ cup. Had a massive resurgence for good reason. Slow-digesting casein protein makes it uniquely filling. Good Culture is the top-tier brand.
